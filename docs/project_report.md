\# Investment Company Industry Analysis Report (2010–2026)



\## Executive Summary



This project analyzes the U.S. investment fund industry using SEC Investment Company Series and Class data from 2010 to 2026. The objective was to understand industry growth, geographic concentration, market leadership, and industry consolidation trends.



Using Python, SQL Server, and Power BI, more than 713,000 records were collected, cleaned, transformed, and analyzed to generate actionable business insights.



The analysis reveals that while the number of investment products increased over time, the number of participating investment companies declined significantly, indicating increasing market concentration and industry consolidation.



\---



\# Business Problem



The investment fund industry has undergone major changes over the past decade. Understanding these changes is important for investors, regulators, and financial institutions.



Key questions addressed include:



\* Is the investment fund industry growing?

\* Are more investment products being introduced?

\* Is the number of investment companies increasing or decreasing?

\* Which states dominate the industry?

\* Which firms control the largest share of products?

\* Is the industry becoming more concentrated?

\# Key Findings



\## 1. Industry Growth



Investment products increased from approximately \*\*39,867 products in 2010\*\* to \*\*43,121 products in 2026\*\*.



\### Insight



The investment industry continues to expand its product offerings despite broader market consolidation.



\---



\## 2. Declining Company Participation



The number of investment companies declined from \*\*2,737 companies in 2010\*\* to \*\*2,044 companies in 2026\*\*.



\### Insight



Approximately 25% of companies disappeared during the study period, suggesting mergers, acquisitions, and market consolidation.



\---



\## 3. Increasing Market Concentration



Average products managed per company increased from \*\*14.6 products\*\* to \*\*21.1 products per company\*\*.



\### Insight



Fewer companies are managing a larger number of products, indicating growing market concentration.



\---



\## 4. Geographic Concentration



The largest concentration of investment products was found in:



1\. Massachusetts

2\. New York

3\. California



\### Insight



The industry remains highly concentrated around major U.S. financial centers.



\---



\## 5. Market Leaders



The largest investment companies include:



\* Goldman Sachs Trust

\* PIMCO Funds

\* John Hancock Funds II

\* JPMorgan Trust I

\* Principal Funds Inc.



\### Insight



A relatively small number of firms manage a substantial share of industry products.



\---



\## 6. Fund Series Analysis



Several fund series account for a disproportionately large number of products.



\### Insight



Product concentration exists not only at the company level but also at the fund series level.

\# Conclusion



The U.S. investment fund industry demonstrates a clear trend toward consolidation.



While the number of investment products continued to grow between 2010 and 2026, the number of participating companies declined significantly. As a result, the average number of products managed per company increased, indicating that a smaller group of firms is controlling a larger share of the market.



This project demonstrates how Python, SQL Server, and Power BI can be combined to build a complete end-to-end analytics solution capable of transforming raw regulatory data into actionable business insights.
